# Referee Report for JASSS

**Manuscript:** `Equilibrium Dynamics of Meaning and Behavioral Sink in Post-Labor Societies: A Stylized Agent-Based Analysis`  
**Files reviewed:** `reports/paper_draft.md`, `models/pathway_a_abm/model.py`, `models/pathway_a_abm/runner.py`, `REPRODUCE.md`, `tests/test_model.py`, committed CSV outputs in `data/simulation/`  
**Recommendation:** `Major Revision`

## Overall assessment

This is substantially stronger than the earlier versions of the manuscript. The core V5 structural correction is real in the code: UBI now maps to `income_support` rather than `role_access`, while role programs restore `role_access` directly. The main ABM headline numbers in the paper are now internally consistent with the committed V5 outputs, and the manuscript is more honest than before about stylization, calibration, and the limits of its validation strategy.

However, I would still recommend **major revision rather than acceptance**. My reason is not that the headline V5 results are numerically wrong; on the contrary, most of them now check out. The remaining barriers are: (1) validation is still implementation-consistency plus post-hoc plausibility, not independent validation; (2) reproducibility is not yet submission-ready because some reported datasets are committed artifacts rather than regenerated by the documented workflow, and the documented test suite does not currently pass as stated; and (3) one supplementary numerical claim about convergence remains false as written.

## Main strengths

1. The manuscript now makes the key V5 mechanism transparent: UBI provides economic buffering without restoring role meaning, while roles restore meaning-generating participation. That is accurately reflected in the code (`model.py`, especially lines 48-50, 131-181, 321-330).
2. The main result sections are much cleaner numerically than before. I checked the principal headline claims in §§2-5 against the committed V5 CSV outputs and found them consistent within rounding.
3. The paper is more appropriately hedged than earlier versions. It repeatedly distinguishes mechanism-finding from prediction and acknowledges the narrow aggression channel, threshold sensitivity, and lack of independent validation.

## Major issues

### 1. Validation remains too weak for acceptance

The manuscript itself now concedes that V5 “validates implementation consistency, not independent replication” (`paper_draft.md`, line 155) and that the historical cases are post-hoc and not prospective tests (`paper_draft.md`, lines 316-322, 375-379). I agree with that self-assessment. The Pathway C comparison is also not an independent validation exercise in a strong sense, because the SD model shares the same SDT-plus-contagion framing and is explicitly calibrated to the Nauru trajectory (`paper_draft.md`, lines 383-387; `models/pathway_c_sd/sd_model.py`, lines 16-35, 167-195).

For JASSS, this does not make the paper worthless, but it does mean the contribution should be framed as a stylized mechanism paper with limited external validation, not as a well-validated account of post-labor social collapse. The current Discussion is mostly honest about this, but the evidentiary standard is still below acceptance level.

### 2. Reproducibility is incomplete in the current repository state

The primary 18,500-run design is documented coherently, and the run counts do match the committed outputs and `REPRODUCE.md`. But the reproduction pipeline is not complete end-to-end.

First, `REPRODUCE.md` explicitly states that the sensitivity and horizon-robustness CSVs were generated in interactive analysis sessions and are merely committed to the repository rather than regenerated by the documented scripts (`REPRODUCE.md`, line 90). That means the paper’s numerical claims in §2.4 and §2.5 are inspectable, but not fully reproducible from a fresh scripted run.

Second, `REPRODUCE.md` states “Expected: 17 tests pass” (`REPRODUCE.md`, line 120), but the current suite does **not** pass as documented. Running `.venv/bin/pytest tests/ -v` yielded 1 failure and 16 passes. The failing test is [`tests/test_model.py`](/Users/cloud/Documents/claude/post-scarcity-abm/tests/test_model.py#L185) asserting `virtual_role == 0.48`, whereas the implementation uses a 0.95 multiplicative decay factor, so `0.5 -> 0.475` is correct under the current code (`model.py`, lines 22 and 193-195). This is a real submission-readiness problem: the documentation claims a green test suite that is not currently green.

### 3. One numerical claim about convergence remains wrong as written

The main text’s convergence claim is defensible: the change in mean `sink_index` between T=80 and T=120 ranges from 0.0009 to 0.0092, which matches `convergence_summary.csv` (`paper_draft.md`, line 157). But the Supplementary Information later states “max Δ < 0.01 to T=240” (`paper_draft.md`, line 487). That is not correct on the committed data: `convergence_summary.csv` shows `delta_120_160 = 0.0144` for the baseline PL=0.80 condition.

This is not a fatal flaw, but it does matter because the paper is now close enough numerically that the remaining problems are exactly these seemingly small but reviewer-visible inconsistencies.

## Minor issues

1. Some repository-facing labels are stale and make the computational narrative look less controlled than the paper suggests. For example, `runner.py` still opens with “Sweep runners for V3 experiments” and contains V2/V4-era comments, despite the manuscript presenting the current implementation as V5.
2. The paper’s JASSS/ODD compliance is improved, but the complete ODD description still appears to live outside the manuscript proper. That is acceptable if clearly treated as supplementary material, but reviewers will expect the main paper and supplement to feel like one coherent submission package.
3. The cultural extrapolation in §3.4-§3.7 is more cautious than before, but examples such as “East Asian, Nordic” (`paper_draft.md`, line 255) still risk reading as broader empirical claims than the model can bear.

## Numerical verification summary

I checked all numerical claims tied to model structure, run counts, thresholds, and reported ABM outputs against the implementation and committed V5 data. The outcome is:

- **Collapse definition:** consistent. The paper defines collapse as final `sink_index > 0.7` (`paper_draft.md`, line 168), and `runner.py` implements exactly that (`runner.py`, line 22). Boundary checks in the data confirm that runs with `sink_index == 0.700` are not flagged collapsed, while `0.705` is.
- **Primary run counts:** consistent. The paper’s 18,500-run total (`paper_draft.md`, lines 138-151) matches `REPRODUCE.md` (`REPRODUCE.md`, lines 55-67, 107) and the committed row counts: 2,250 + 3,000 + 3,600 + 3,600 + 100 + 4,500 + 1,000 + 450.
- **Threshold sweep (§3.1):** verified. Baseline collapse rises from 2% at PL=0.80 to 86% at PL=0.90 and 100% at PL=0.95, with mean sink 0.629, 0.736, and 0.788 respectively.
- **Extreme-displacement intervention ranking (§3.5):** verified. At PL=0.95 in `sweep6_full_grid.csv`, `all_bundle` has sink 0.115 and 0% collapse; `roles` 0.472 and 0%; `ubi_only` 0.743 and 87.3%; `baseline` 0.790 and 100%.
- **`collapsed_frac` versus run-level collapse (§3.1, §3.5, Conclusion):** verified. The manuscript’s distinction is correct: e.g. `ubi_only` at PL=0.95 has mean `collapsed_frac` 0.325 but run-level collapse 87.3%.
- **Collectivism moderation (§3.4):** verified. Under `ubi_only` at PL=0.95, collapse falls from 97% to 31% as collectivism rises from 0.0 to 1.0, and sink falls from 0.769 to 0.684.
- **Virtual-world sweep (§3.2):** verified within rounding. Baseline PL=0.95 goes from 100% collapse, sink 0.793 at quality 0.0 to 0% collapse, sink 0.520 at quality 1.0.
- **Clean speed comparison (§3.3):** verified. At PL=0.95, baseline sink is 0.638 under rapid versus 0.780 under gradual at t+10 after target, narrowing to 0.777 versus 0.790 by t+40.
- **Aggressor prevalence:** verified. The manuscript’s 1.6% (time-series baseline, step 80) and 3.3% (full-grid baseline, PL=0.95) claims match the committed outputs.

Outside those ABM checks, the Pathway C sentence claiming Nauru-like sink about 0.71 and Gulf-like sink below 0.15 is numerically consistent if evaluated at the SD model’s t=30 checkpoint, but it should still be treated as implementation-consistency evidence rather than strong cross-method validation.

## Recommendation

I recommend **Major Revision**.

The manuscript is now much closer to a publishable stylized ABM paper than it was in earlier iterations. The V5 code-paper consistency problem is largely fixed. But the paper is not yet ready for acceptance because the validation story remains weak, the reproduction story is incomplete, and one documented numerical claim is still false. A revised submission that (1) corrects the convergence statement, (2) makes the documented test/reproduction pipeline fully green, and (3) positions the validation claims more conservatively could plausibly move into minor-revision territory.
